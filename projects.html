Sunrise Residential Solar Array

  • Location: Quezon City, Metro Manila

  • System Size: 10 kW

  • Annual Energy Output: 14,600 kWh

  • Total Expenses: ₱1,320,000

  • Completion Time: 3 weeks

  • Start Date: October 1, 2024

  • Completion Date: October 22, 2024

This project involves installing a 10 kW solar system on a single-family home in Quezon City, designed to produce 14,600 kWh of clean energy per year. The system will reduce the homeowner's electricity bills while providing long-term energy savings. The total cost, including equipment, installation, and permits, is estimated at ₱1,320,000. With an installation timeline of 3 weeks, the homeowner will start seeing savings immediately. The investment will likely pay off within 7-10 years.

Green Valley Community Solar Farm

  • Location: Batangas

  • System Size: 200 kW

  • Annual Energy Output: 292,000 kWh

  • Total Expenses: ₱19,188,000

  • Completion Time: 4 months

  • Start Date: November 1, 2024

  • Completion Date: March 1, 2025

Located in Batangas, this 200 kW community solar farm will serve 40 households, providing them with affordable, renewable energy. The array is expected to generate 292,000 kWh annually, significantly lowering electricity bills for participants. The total project cost is ₱19,188,000, covering equipment, labor, and permitting. Completion is expected within 4 months, with households benefiting from both cost savings and a reduced carbon footprint. This project promotes a community-based approach to sustainable energy in the region.

Baguio Hills Resort Solar Initiative

  • Location: Baguio City, Benguet

  • System Size: 80 kW

  • Annual Energy Output: 116,800 kWh

  • Total Expenses: ₱7,700,000

  • Completion Time: 6 weeks

  • Start Date: October 20, 2024

  • Completion Date: December 1, 2024

This project will install an 80 kW solar system for a resort in the highlands of Baguio City. The system will generate 116,800 kWh annually, significantly reducing the resort’s reliance on grid electricity and improving its sustainability. With a total expense of ₱7,700,000, the installation will be completed within 6 weeks, providing long-term savings on energy costs and promoting eco-tourism in the region. The resort will also benefit from lower operational costs and enhanced environmental responsibility.
